Finance Minister Rt Hon Bill Birch said today he was disappointed that the 0 to 2 per cent underlying inflation target range outlined in the Policy Targets Agreement had been breached.

"This is the second breach of the target since price stability was achieved, and follows the initial breach in the June 1995 quarter," Mr Birch said.

"However, the breach of the target is small and should not affect inflation expectations  
in the wider community."

Mr Birch said he would seek an explanation from the Governor of the Reserve Bank  
on:

- what factors led to the breach of the target range;
- what action the Governor is taking to direct inflation back within the range;
- when the Governor expects underlying inflation to return to the target range; and
- what steps the Governor will take to minimise the possibility of a further breach.

"As previously, I will be seeking a report from the non-executive directors of the  
Reserve Bank on the Governor's performance," he said.
