The Government is pleased to announce the following appointments to the Board of Te Mangai Paho, the Maori Broadcasting Funding Agency:

Bill Nathan a current Board member has been appointed as its Deputy Chairperson replacing Professor Timoti Karetu whose term expired on 18 August 1996. Mr Nathan is of Te Atiawa/Ngati Kahungunu and Ngai Tahu origin. Prior to his retirement from the New Zealand Army, Mr Nathan held the rank of Colonel.

Toby Curtis has been reappointed as an ordinary member of the Board. He is of Ngati Pikiao origin. Mr Curtis has had valuable experience in the administration of Maori broadcasting. He is currently the Dean of the Faculty of Te Ara Poutama, Auckland Institute of Technology.

Hekia Parata and Ian Cormack have been appointed as new members. Ms Parata is of Ngati Porou and Ngai Tahu origin. She has extensive experience with Government policy processes and Maori issues. Mr Cormack is of Ngai Tahu origin and has had wide experience in Maori education, administration and consultation.

Other members of the Board are Hiwi Tauroa CMG, Chairperson, and Frank Solomon.
