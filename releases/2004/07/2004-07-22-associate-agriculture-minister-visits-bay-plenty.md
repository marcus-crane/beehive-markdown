[![Associate Agriculture Minister Damien O'Connor meets with Fonterra's response team in the flood-stricken Bay of Plenty.](/sites/default/files/styles/embed_medium/public/images/DSCF1090_Main.jpg?itok=8LONNO21)](/media/56)

Associate Agriculture Minister Damien O'Connor will today visit the flood stricken Bay of Plenty.

Joining Waiariki MP Mita Ririnui and Maori Affairs Minister Parekura Horomia in the area, Mr O'Connor will view damage to agricultural land, business and infrastructure, and meet with local farmers.

Mr O'Connor's timetable for the day is as follows:

09:15- Operations Committee briefing on flood impact - Environment Bay of Plenty offices, Quay Street, Whakatane

11:00- Meet with the mayors of affected regions - Environment Bay of Plenty.

12:00- Lunch meeting with Federated Farmers representatives - Environment Bay of Plenty.

12:30 – Visit control centre set up by Fed Farmers at Dexcel.

12:45 – Tour of the district to meet affected farmers.

14:00 - Meet with Fonterra staff to view the Diary Factory and discuss flood impact on the company - Fonterra site, Edgecombe.

14:30 -Meet with Tony Hawken and Zespri staff - Eastpack Ltd, West Bank Road, Edgecombe.

Mr O'Connor said the government was committed to helping those caught by the flooding; to do so it needed to fully grasp its effects.

"The best way to do that is to view the damage first hand and to talk to people on the ground."
