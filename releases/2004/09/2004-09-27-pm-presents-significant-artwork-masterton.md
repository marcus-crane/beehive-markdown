Prime Minister and Minister for Arts, Culture and Heritage, Helen Clark, today presented a significant New Zealand painting to the people of Masterton.

The Toss Woollaston work, simply titled "Landscape", is thought to date from around 1970. Purchased by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade, it has been hung until now in the New Zealand Embassy in Brussels.

The oil painting is one of 59 artworks formerly held in New Zealand's overseas posts which are being gifted to 24 galleries and museums from Whangarei to Invercargill.

Helen Clark this afternoon presented the work to Tracy Puklowski, the Director of Aratoi, the Wairarapa Museum of Art and History in Masterton.

"This museum has now been gifted two significant paintings from the former collection of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade, and I am delighted that the people of the Wairarapa, and their visitors, will be able to view them," said Helen Clark.

"The Wairarapa Museum holds three portraits by Woollaston, but no landscapes. It has a very limited acquisitions fund. This work will enlarge its representation of New Zealand landscape paintings. The Associate Arts Minister Judith Tizard has previously presented Aratoi with a significant John Weeks landscape, which had also been part of the MFAT collection."

"These are powerful and important works and will give a new focus to the fine collections which are already held and displayed in Aratoi," said Helen Clark.
