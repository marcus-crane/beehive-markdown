Thirty-three of the 58 constables of Wing 219 graduating today from the Police College have been posted to the Auckland area.

Police Minister George Hawkins said of the remaining new constables, two were headed for Northland, 10 to other North Island districts and 13 to the South Island.

Mr Hawkins said Kaitaia will receive one new constable, Dargaville one, Auckland Central station two, Auckland City two, Avondale eight, Howick one, Henderson one, Mt Wellington two, Manurewa one, Newmarket four, Papakura seven, Takapuna five, Huntly one, Hamilton one, Taumaranui two, Taupo one, Wanganui two, Hawera two, Palmerston North one, Blenheim one, Greymouth one, Queenstown one, Christchurch four, Invercargill one, and Dunedin five.

The 46 men and 12 women graduating from Wing 219 ranged in age from 23 to 47 years, with the average age being 30.

They come from a wide variety of backgrounds and occupations including the Army and Navy, as well as teaching, nursing, farming, business and trade backgrounds. Many had family connections within the police.

Wing 219 patron was disabled climber Mark Inglis, who had kept in touch with the Wing through email even while on a climbing expedition in the Himalayas, Mr Hawkins said.
