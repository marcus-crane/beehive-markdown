Justice Minister Judith Collins has announced New Plymouth lawyer John Auld as the new Chairperson of the Real Estate Agents Authority (REAA).

The REAA is an independent Crown entity that regulates New Zealand’s real estate industry, promotes a high standard of service and professionalism in the industry and helps protect buyers and sellers of property.

“I congratulate Mr Auld on his appointment as Chair,” Ms Collins says.

“Mr Auld’s legal acumen and governance experience equip him well to take on the challenge of leading the Authority.”

Mr Auld will take over from the current Chair, Ms Kristy McDonald QC, who has served in the position since 2009.

Ms Collins acknowledges Ms McDonald for her role in successfully establishing the Authority and for her excellent leadership.

Mr Auld will take up the position for a one year term from 6 August 2013.

**Biography**:

Mr Auld has been a member of the REAA since its establishment in 2009. He is an experienced commercial and property lawyer and also has wide experience in corporate governance. Mr Auld is a past President of the Taranaki District Law Society and served as a Disputes Tribunal Referee from 2009 to 2012. He was formerly Deputy Chair of the Taranaki District Health Board and Deputy Chair of the Western Institute of Technology. Mr Auld is currently Chair of Port Taranaki.
