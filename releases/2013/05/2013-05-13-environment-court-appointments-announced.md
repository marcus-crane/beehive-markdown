Attorney-General Christopher Finlayson today announced the appointment of Jim Hodges and Eileen von Dadelszen as Commissioners of the Environment Court, John Illingsworth as a Deputy Commissioner and the reappointment of David Bunting and Russell Howie as Commissioners, each for a five year term.

Mr Hodges is an Auckland based civil and environmental engineer with major project management experience. He was the Chief Engineer of Watercare Services from 2007 to 2011 and worked on resource management issues for URS (NZ) Ltd for more than 15 years previously. Mr Hodges is an independent Resource Management Act (RMA) Commissioner and a Chartered Professional Engineer.

Mrs von Dadelszen has 17 years’ experience as a councillor on the Hawke’s Bay Regional Council. She has held the position of chair and deputy chair. She is also a resource management consultant and planner. In 2009 Mrs von Dadelszen was awarded the New Zealand Planning Institute’s Distinguished Service Award. She is an independent RMA Chair and Commissioner.

Mr Illingsworth is an engineer by profession and a former director and senior manager of aggregate, construction, and land development firms. He is currently involved in an aquaculture project at Ruakaka. He is a Chartered Professional Engineer and lives in Cambridge.

Mr Bunting, of Wellington, has served as a part-time Commissioner since 2007. He has previous experience as an international business development manager for Opus International with a focus on physical infrastructure development projects. He is a Fellow of the Institute of Professional Engineers.

Mr Howie is a civil engineer who, immediately prior to his appointment in 2001, had been in independent RMA Commissioner for Wellington local authorities and a consultant on water resources management. Mr Howie has worked as a part-time Commissioner since 2001. He is also a Fellow of the Institute of Professional Engineers.

The Environment Court considers applications and appeals made under the RMA and has wide ranging jurisdiction under the Act. It also has significant jurisdiction under other legislation.
