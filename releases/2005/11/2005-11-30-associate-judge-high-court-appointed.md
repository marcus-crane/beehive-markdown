Attorney-General David Parker today announced the appointment of Jeremy Paul Doogue as an Associate Judge of the High Court.

Associate Judge Doogue graduated from Auckland University School of Law and joined the firm McCaw Lewis Chapman in Hamilton where he was a partner for eight years. He commenced practice as a barrister in 1987.

In 1996 he was appointed to the District Court bench and was Executive Judge for the Northern Region from 1999 to 2002. Since that time Associate Judge Doogue has held a number of acting appointments, the most recent as a temporary Associate Judge of the High Court.

Associate Judge Doogue served on the Rules Committee from 1998 until 2002. He has also been a member of the Save the Children Fund since 1988, serving three years as a Branch President for that organisation.
