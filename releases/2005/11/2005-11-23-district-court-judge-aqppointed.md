Attorney-General David Parker has announced the appointment of Roy Wade as a District Court Judge.

Mr Wade, of Auckland, will be sworn in on 30 November and will sit at Manukau District Court.

Mr Wade qualified in Britain by serving articles of clerkship prior to his admission as a solicitor in 1969. He then worked for nine years in a small rural practice undertaking a variety of legal work, including prosecuting for the police. He spent 13 years as a barrister in Birmingham practising in criminal law, general common law, family and tribunal work before immigrating to New Zealand.

He joined Meredith Connell in 1994 and became Senior Crown Prosecutor, engaged exclusively in criminal work.
