Attorney-General David Parker has announced the appointment of Dominic Flatley as a Family Court Judge.

Mr Flatley, from Christchurch, will be sworn in on 8 December and will sit in Invercargill.

Mr Flatley was admitted to the bar in 1987 and has practised predominantly in family law since 1992. He is a partner of Layburn Hodgins and heads the firm's litigation team. Before joining Layburn Hodgins in 2000 he had spent three years in sole practice and five years with Cameron & Company.

Mr Flatley was appointed a Child Support Review Officer for IRD in 1996, to the Residence Appeal Authority (now the Residence Review Board) in 1999 and to the Removal Review Authority in 2003.

He is a founding trustee of the Fathering the Future Trust and has taken a keen interest in parenting education. Mr Flatley holds a BA, LLB and Master of Education (with Distinction).
