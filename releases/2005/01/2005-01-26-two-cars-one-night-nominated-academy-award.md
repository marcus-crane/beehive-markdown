Associate Arts, Culture and Heritage Minister Judith Tizard says today's [Academy Award nomination](http://www.oscars.com/nominees/nom_34256.html) for *Two Cars, One Night*, is a great achievement for Taika Waititi, its New Zealand writer and director.

"I congratulate Taika on his success with Two Cars, One Night and wish him all the best for these awards," Judith Tizard said.

"The nomination is another boost for the New Zealand film industry, and further evidence that we are up there with the world when it comes to film-making."

*Two Cars, One Night* has been nominated for an Academy Awardâ„¢ in the Short Film Live Action category, it was announced in Los Angeles today.

Produced by Ainsley Gardiner and Catherine Fitzgerald, the film tells the story of first love when two children develop a relationship as they wait for their parents in the carpark of a rural pub.

Taika and Ainsely are currently at the Sundance Film Festival where their second short film *Tama Tu* has been selected in competition.

*Two Cars, One Night* was financed by the New Zealand Film Commission and executive produced by Vanessa Alexander. The New Zealand Film Commission is assisting the filmmakers to attend the Academy Awards.

Oscar winners will be announced at the 77th Annual Academy Awards at the Kodak Theatre in Hollywood on Sunday February 27, 2005 at 5pm Los Angeles time.
