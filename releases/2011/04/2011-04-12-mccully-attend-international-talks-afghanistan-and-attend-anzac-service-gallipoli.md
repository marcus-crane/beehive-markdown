Foreign Affairs Minister, Murray McCully, departs today for a visit to Europe and the Middle East.

The Minister will join an international Foreign Ministers meeting in Berlin on Afghanistan and hold bilateral talks in Europe and the Middle East. He’ll attend the 96th anniversary of the Gallipoli campaign in Turkey.

Mr McCully said the Berlin meeting with International Security Assistance Force (ISAF) nations will further progress work on how best to transition provinces to Afghan leadership.

“New Zealand has recently been given the important task of guiding the Bamyan province to Afghan leadership of security, development and governance. I will be exchanging strategies and ideas with the other ISAF nations and the Afghan government,” said Mr McCully.

After the Berlin meeting the Minister will travel to Croatia, the United Arab Emirates, Saudi Arabia, and Turkey. He will also attend a meeting of the Commonwealth Action Group (CMAG) in London.

Mr McCully will represent the country at the 96th anniversary of the Gallipoli campaign. He will speak at the Dawn Service at the ANZAC Commemorative Site and the New Zealand Memorial Service at Chunuk Bair. He will attend the Australian Memorial Service at Lone Pine and the Turkish 57th Regiment Memorial Service.
