Commerce Minister Simon Power today announced the appointment of Elizabeth Hopkins as a member and James Hill as Deputy Chair of the Testing Laboratory Registration Council.

Ms Hopkins has been appointed for a term of three years, expiring on 16 May 2013.

Mr Hill's appointment coincides with his existing term as a member of the council, due to expire on 31 March 2012.

"I'm delighted that Mr Hill has agreed to take on the role of Deputy Chair, and I welcome Ms Hopkins who brings commercial science expertise to the council," Mr Power said.

He also thanked Jo Brosnahan for her services to the council.

**Background**

Elizabeth Hopkins will succeed TLR Council member Jo Brosnahan.  Ms Hopkins works closely with NZBio, Innate Therapeutics, Canterbury Polytechnic Institute of Technology, the Foundation for Research Science and Technology, and MigCo Pharmaceuticals.  She has worked in several areas of commercial science, including pharmaceuticals, nutraceuticals, medical devices, cosmetics, and food, and was previously CEO of Equities/ Keratec Ltd.

James Hill will replace incumbent Deputy Chair Dr Richard Janes, who will remain a TLR Council member.  Mr Hill chairs the council's Audit and Risk Committee and is a director on a number of listed and unlisted companies. He chairs the boards of Manukau Water, Seafood Processors, Sealegs Corporation, and CDP Group. His past directorships include Metrowater, the  Yellow Bus Company, and Transit NZ. He was the Managing Partner of KPMG Auckland from 1994 to 2002.
