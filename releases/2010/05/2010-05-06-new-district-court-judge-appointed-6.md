Rotorua lawyer Alayne Catherine Wills has been appointed a District Court Judge with a Family Court warrant, Attorney-General Christopher Finlayson announced today. 

Ms Wills is a partner at Rotorua firm East Brewster.  For the last few years she has predominantly worked in the family law area, but has a general litigation background. She has been a faculty member of the New Zealand Law Society litigation skills programme for many years, a member of the Maori Consultative Group of the Family Law section, and an inaugural member of Te Hunga Roia Maori.  She has acted as Lawyer for the Child since 1985 and is appointed by the Court as Counsel to Assist in complex cases.  A member of the panel of lawyers appointed as Counsel for the Subject Person in PPPR cases, she is also one of the panel of Lawyers appointed to undertake Hague Convention work. 

Ms Wills is of Ngai Tahu descent and maintains her involvement with her runanga in Te Wai Pounamu.  She has also been involved with kohanga administration and is a Trustee of the Rotorua District Community Law Centre.

Ms Wills will be sworn in at Rotorua on 24 May 2010 and will sit in Tauranga.
