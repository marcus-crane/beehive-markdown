Earthquake recovery Minister Gerry Brownlee has today announced the final member of the Canterbury Earthquake Recovery Commission.

Arihia Bennett MNZM will be appointed to the seven-member Commission, which will first meet after the conclusion of local body elections this month. Ms Bennett has extensive experience in social work and the business sector.

The Commission was established by the Canterbury Earthquake Response and Recovery Act 2010, passed unanimously by Parliament in September.

The Commission will be chaired by outgoing Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry Director General Murray Sherwin. Other members of the Commission are Dame Margaret Bazley, a representative of Environment Canterbury, and David Hopkins, an earthquake engineering expert.

The remaining commissioners will be the mayors of Christchurch City and the Selwyn and Waimakariri Districts elected at the 9 October local body elections.

**Biography**

Arihia Bennett (Ngai Tahu, Ngati Porou) is a social worker with a distinguished career in community and iwi development, and in the ecotourism sector. She has extensive experience in corporate governance, having held Directorships with Ngai Tahu, the Social Workers Registration Board and Barnardos, and is currently the Regional Manager, Te Waipounamu for Barnardos.

Ms Bennett was made a Member of the New Zealand Order of Merit in 2008 for services to Maori and the community. She lives in Kaiapoi.
