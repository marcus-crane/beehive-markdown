Whangarei barrister Gerard Thomas Winter has been appointed a District Court Judge, Attorney-General Christopher Finlayson announced today. 

Mr Winter was admitted to the bar in 1982. He is a senior lawyer, advocate, mediator and Jurist with 28 years experience in civilian and military courts in New Zealand and overseas. From 2003 to 2008, Mr Winter was a High Court Judge in Fiji. In 2008 he prepared and advocated a National Report for the Universal Periodic Review hearings before the Human Rights Council, Geneva. In 2009, Mr. Winter was appointed a Royal Law Commissioner, for the Kingdom of Tonga and Legal Adviser on Constitutional and Political reforms, following work in the Republic of Nauru as a UNDP Constitutional Adviser. 

From 1995 to 2003 Mr. Winter was in sole practice in Whangarei and Auckland. He is a Senior Crown Counsel panel member for Northland. A founding member of the New Zealand Law Society Family Law Section, Mr. Winter was a senior Counsel for the Child and Youth Advocate. He took a leading role in the establishment of the ‘155' community law centre in Whangarei.

Mr Winter will be sworn in at Whangarei on 30 April 2010 and will sit in Manukau.
