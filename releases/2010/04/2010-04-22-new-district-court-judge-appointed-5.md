Wellington Crown Counsel Christina Inglis has been appointed a District Court Judge, Attorney-General Christopher Finlayson announced today. 

Ms Inglis was admitted to the bar in 1993. She has been with Crown Law since 1996, where she currently heads the Human Rights Team. She has also worked with various other teams, including Commercial, Employment, Social Services, Treaty Issues and Criminal. She began her legal career with the Ministry of Transport. Ms Inglis has an MA (Hons) from Canterbury University and an LLM (Hons) from Victoria University. 

Ms Inglis has been a member of Wellington District Law Society Committees and is a long-standing member of the Wellington Women Lawyers' Association. Ms Inglis is the daughter of Judge BD Inglis QC, who died two years ago.

She will be sworn in at Wellington on 21 May 2010 and will sit in Manukau.
