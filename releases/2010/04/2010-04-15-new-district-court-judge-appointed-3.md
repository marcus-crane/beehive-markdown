Nelson Solicitor Raymond Glen Marshall has been appointed a District Court Judge, Attorney-General Christopher Finlayson announced today. 

Mr Marshall was admitted to the bar in 1980. Prior to 1986 Mr Marshall was engaged in all aspects of litigation, civil, family, criminal, and tribunal work, and became an Associate of Tanner, Fitzgerald and Getty in Hamilton. From 1986 to 2001 he was a Barrister sole in Hamilton, concentrating on jury trials. 

In 1994 he was appointed a member of the Crown Prosecutors Panel in Hamilton and from 1990 to 1998 he was a youth advocate.  In 2001 he was appointed Senior Prosecutor with Almao Kellaway. Since 2002 he has been Crown Solicitor for Tasman and a partner in Pitt and Moore in Nelson. He has also served as a legal advisor to the Medical Practitioners Complaints Assessment Committees (1996-2001) and review officer for HIH Workable Ltd (1999-2001).

Mr Marshall will be sworn in at Nelson on 23 April 2010 and will sit in Hamilton.
