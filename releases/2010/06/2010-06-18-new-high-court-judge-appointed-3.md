Lawyer Timothy Charles Brewer has been appointed a Judge of the High Court, Attorney-General Christopher Finlayson announced today. 

Mr Brewer graduated LLB (Hons) (First Class) from Victoria University of Wellington in 1979. Between 1988 and 2010 he was the Crown Solicitor for New Plymouth, and was Law Commissioner between the years 1997 and 2001. 

After serving in the Territorial Force of the New Zealand Army, he retired with the rank of Brigadier in 2009. In 2003 his service was recognised when he was appointed an Officer of the New Zealand Order of Merit.

From 2004 to 2009 Mr Brewer held the position of Judge-Advocate.  He was most recently the Senior Litigation Partner at Auld Brewer Mazenbarb & McEwen and was appointed a Judge of the Court Martial of New Zealand in 2009.

The new judge will sit in Auckland, commencing on 1 July 2010.
