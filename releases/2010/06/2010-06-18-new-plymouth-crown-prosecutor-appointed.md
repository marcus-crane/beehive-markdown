Lawyer Cherie Evelyn Clarke has been appointed the new Crown Solicitor at New Plymouth, Attorney-General Christopher Finlayson announced today.

Ms Clarke will replace Tim Brewer, who has held the Crown Solicitor's warrant since 1988.

Ms Clarke graduated from the University of Canterbury and began her career as a law clerk at the Raymond Donnelly & Co, the Christchurch Crown Solicitor's Office.

In 1997 Ms Clarke joined the independent bar. During this time she was a committee member of the Canterbury Criminal Bar Association and of the Canterbury District Law Society's Public Relations Committee.

Between 1999 and 2004 Ms Clarke returned to Crown prosecution work, this time in Wellington, with the firm Luke Cunningham & Clere. In 2005 she joined Auld Brewer Mazengarb & McEwen, the New Plymouth Crown Solicitor's Office, as an associate, becoming a partner in 2006.
