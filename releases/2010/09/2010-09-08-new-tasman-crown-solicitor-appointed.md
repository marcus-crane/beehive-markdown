Mark Anthony O'Donoghue has been appointed Crown Solicitor for Tasman, Attorney-General Christopher Finlayson announced today. Mr O'Donoghue takes over the role of Crown Solicitor for Tasman from Glen Marshall, following his appointment to the District Court.Â 

Mr O'Donoghue graduated from Victoria University of Wellington in 1981. Since 1986 Mr O'Donoghue has been a partner with Luke Cunningham & Clere, Crown Solicitors at Wellington, and has appeared as a prosecutor for the Crown in the Court of Appeal, High Court and District Court. He has also appeared in the Family Court and before various administrative Tribunals.

In addition, Mr O'Donoghue has served on the Wellington District Law Society District Court Committee (1994) and the District Law Society High Court Committee (1992-3). He also lectures regularly at the Royal New Zealand Police College.

The appointment will take effect from 11 October 2010. Mr O'Donoghue will be establishing a new firm in Nelson to house the warrant.
