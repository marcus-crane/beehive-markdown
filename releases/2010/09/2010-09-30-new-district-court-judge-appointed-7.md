Whakatane barrister Sarah Lindsay has been appointed a District Court Judge with a Family Court warrant, Attorney-General Christopher Finlayson announced today.

Sarah Lindsay was admitted to the Bar in 1991. She worked in Opotiki for Potts & Hodgson, before commencing with Davenports, Takapuna office, with a brief period in the Ombudsman's office.

In 1998, after a stint as a barrister, she joined Davys Burton in Rotorua as an Associate assisting with leading the Family law team.  From 2000 to 2003 she was with East Brewster, Rotorua. 

In 2004 Ms Lindsay and her family returned to home to the Eastern Bay of Plenty, based in Whakatane but also regularly working in the Opotiki Court both the Family and Youth Court jurisdictions. Since 2006 she has been a Barrister Sole specialising in all aspects of Family Law and Youth Advocacy.  She was appointed Lawyer for the child in 1998 and since then has also appeared for disadvantaged subject persons as Counsel to Assist and also since 2004 to the Youth Advocate list.  Ms Lindsay  has undertaken LEADR training and co-presented a paper in respect of CYFS proceedings at the NZ Family Law conference in 2009.

She will be sworn in at Whakatane on 12 November 2010 and will sit in Whangarei.
