Building and Construction Minister Maurice Williamson has announced the appointment of Jon Williams to []()[]()the Chartered Professional Engineers Council.

"Mr Williams brings over 20 years of experience in the consulting engineering profession to this role.  He has extensive experience of the competency assessment of chartered engineers," Mr Williamson says.

The Chartered Professional Engineers Council consists of six to eight members nominated by the Institution of Professional Engineers of New Zealand, the Association of Consulting Engineers of New Zealand, and the Minister for Building and Construction. 

Mr Williams was nominated by Association of Consulting Engineers and has been appointed for a term of five years.  He replaces Peter Smith, the Association's pervious nomination, who decided not to seek reappointment when his term expired. 

"Mr Smith has served on the Council since 2003 and I thank him for his valuable contribution," Mr Williamson says. 

The Chartered Professional Engineers Council is a statutory body established under the Chartered Professional Engineers Act 2002. 

The Council reviews and approves rules about minimum standards of competence and ethical conduct, and considers appeals by engineers about registration decisions and disciplinary matters.  It also reviews and reports to the Minister on the performance of the registration authority.

For more information about Chartered Professional Engineers Council visit: [www.cpec.org.nz](http://www.cpec.org.nz/)
