The Minister of Foreign Affairs and Trade, Rt Hon Don McKinnon will be meeting with Mr Fatchett for talks, Friday 19 September, 11.30am to 12.00pm.

As Minister for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs, Mr Fatchett has a wide range of responsibilities embracing the Middle East and North Africa, the Far East and Pacific including Hong Kong, and South and South East Asia. In addition Mr Fatchett is responsible for export promotion, cultural relations, the environment, science and energy and general administration services of the Foreign and Commonwealth Office.

Mr McKinnon and Mr Fatchett will be available for comment/pics following their talks at 12.00pm.

Mr McKinnon is hosting the meeting in his office, Suite 6.3, Beehive.

**DEREK FATCHETT, MP**

Derek Fatchett MP - Derek John Fatchett - Minister of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs, was born on 22 August 1945 and educated at Lincoln School, Birmingham University and the London School of Economics. He was originally involved in local government but as a Member of Parliament (MP) has represented the constituency of Leeds Central since 1983. He came into politics after an academic career, which included lecturing in Industrial Relations at Leeds University. As Minister of State, Mr Fatchett has a wide range of responsibilities, embracing the Middle East and North Africa, the Far East and Pacific including Hong Kong, and South and South East Asia. In addition to these, Mr Fatchett is responsible for export promotion, cultural relations, the environment, science and energy and general and administration services of the Foreign and Commonwealth Office. Mr Fatchett had solid experience in opposition, as spokesperson on foreign and Commonwealth affairs from 1995-1997 and on defence from 1994-1995. IIn addition, he was opposition spokesman on trade and industry from 1992-1994, spokesman on employment and training from 1988-1992 and spokesperson on education from 1987-1988. He is a former Opposition Whip and is a member of the Tribune Group within the Labour Party. Mr Fatchett has wide interests beyond his current brief and these include the economy, industry, education and health. Mr Fatchett, who is married with two children, lists his leisure pursuits as sport, reading and the theatre.
