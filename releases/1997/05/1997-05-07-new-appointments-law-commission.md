The Minister of Justice, Hon D.A.M. Graham, today announced that Donald Frederick Dugdale and Denese Letitia Henare have been appointed to the Law Commission.

Ms Henare's appointment is for three years; Mr Dugdale's for five years.

Ms Henare graduated LLB from the University of Auckland in 1974 and was admitted to the bar the same year. She gained a Diploma in Air and Space Law at University College, London, in 1978.

In 1990 she was awarded the New Zealand Commemoration Medal in recognition of services to NZ.

Ms Henare, a sole practitioner in Auckland, is External Co-ordinator - law and Legal Issues Paper - Graduate School of Business, Auckland University.

She is a Director of the Northern Regional Health Authority, the Transitional Health Authority and Tainui Corporation Limited and Auditor of the NZ Universities Academic Unit.

Mr Dugdale, formerly a senior partner in an Auckland law firm, graduated LLB from the University of Auckland in 1956 and was admitted to the Bar that year.

He is a Past President of the Auckland District Law Society and a former Vice-President of the NZ Law Society. He is a former Member of the Contracts and Commercial law Reform Committee, the NZ Council of Law Reporting and the Council of Legal Education.
