The Minister of Justice, Hon D.A.M. Graham, today announced the appointment of Auckland Barrister David Graham Mather as a District Court Judge.

Judge Mather will be sworn in at the Henderson District Court on 26 February and will sit in the Family Court at Otahuhu from 31 March.

Judge Mather graduated BA from Auckland University in 1970 and LL.B (Hons) in 1972. He was Senior Scholar in Law in 1971 and was awarded the Spencer Mason Travelling Scholarship in 1972. He graduated LL.M from Queen's University, Ontario, in 1973. He was admitted to the Bar in 1972.

He practised in Auckland for three different legal firms before becoming Barrister Sole in 1986 specialising in general litigation and advice primarily in the civil and family areas but including some criminal work, professional disciplinary briefs, accident compensation and arbitration.

In recent years Judge Mather has had a particular interest in mediation and he has practised as a mediator.

He is a former faculty member of the NZ Law Society's litigation skills course and Convenor of the Law in Schools Committee.

Judge Mather is a cost revisor for the Auckland District Law Society and he has been a member of various committees including legal aid, family law, District Court, library and the benevolent fund.

He is a former guest tutor at the Institute of Professional Legal Studies and a former adjudicator under the Residential Tenancies Act.

He is a legal assessor for the Dentist's Disciplinary Tribunal, a member of the Medical Misadventure Advisory Committee, an associate of the Arbitrators and Mediators Institute, a member of the NZ Chapter of LEADR (Lawyers Engaged in Alternative Dispute Resolution) and a member of LEADR's advanced panel of mediators.
