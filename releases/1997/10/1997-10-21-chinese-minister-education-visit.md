Mr Zhu Kaixuan, the Minister of Education, People's Republic of China will visit later this month to examine aspects of the New Zealand education system, Education Minister Wyatt Creech announced today.

This is the first visit to New Zealand by a Minister of Education from China.

Mr Zhu Kaixuan and his delegation will be investigating tertiary education, distance learning and Chinese language teaching in particular.

"This year marks the 25th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between our two countries. The visit of Minister Zhu Kaixuan, represents a new stage of co-operation and dialogue on education issues," Mr Creech said.

"Increased educational contacts will improve our mutual understanding in this area and raise levels of cultural awareness.

"At a broader level, strong education links with overseas countries help New Zealand's international competitiveness by educating the future workforce to be aware of cultural diversity, other languages and the global economic and social forces shaping society," Mr Creech said.

Mr Zhu Kaixuan arrives for five days from Sunday 26 October.

Minister Zhu will meet the Minister of Education, Hon Wyatt Creech, and Ministry of Education officials during his visit. He will also make a courtesy call on Deputy Prime Minister Winston Peters.

The Minister will visit Lincoln, Otago, Victoria and Auckland Universities, Auckland Institute of Technology and the Open Polytechnic.

The delegation will also meet the students and staff at Outram Rural School in the Taieri Plains, Hutt Valley High School and Newton Central School in Auckland.
