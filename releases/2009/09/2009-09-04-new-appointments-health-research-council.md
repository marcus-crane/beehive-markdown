The Health Minister Tony Ryall has announced the founder of Christchurch's Skope Industries, Mr Robert Stewart as new chair of the Health Research Council. 

"Mr Stewart is a successful businessman, innovator and philanthropist. He also has a good understanding of the research funding environment being President of the independent Canterbury Medical Research Foundation."

Mr Stewart is joined on the Board by:

- Professor Richard Beasley, awarded CNZM in 2008 for services to medical research
- Ms Kath Fox, Chief Executive of Richmond New Zealand
- Dr Matire Harwood, Clinical Director of Tamaki Healthcare Primary Health Organisation
- Ms Ele Ludemann, farmer, and past Deputy Chair of Waitaki District Health Services Ltd
- Professor Richie Poulton,  Director of the Dunedin Multidisciplinary Health and Development Study
- Dr Conway Powell, currently consults mainly in the health sector on primary health care development
- Professor Tony Reeve, Director of the Cancer Genetics Laboratory and Otago Genomics Facility in Dunedin
- Professor Linda Smith, Pro Vice-Chancellor Māori at the University of Waikato
- Associate Professor Susan Stott, based at the Department of Surgery, Faculty of Medical and Health Sciences, University of Auckland

Mr Ryall says "Five members, including four researcher members, have current experience on the Board ensuring continuity of governance."

Mr Ryall also thanked the retiring members for their work on the board, and former Chair, Professor Graeme Fraser for his leadership in recent years.
