Attorney-General Christopher Finlayson has announced the appointment of four new lay members to the High Court, under the provisions of the Commerce Act.

The new members are:

\- Professor David Round, Professor of Economics, and Director of the Centre for Regulation and Market Analysis in the School of Commerce at the University of South Australia;

\- Professor Glenn Boyle of the University of Canterbury Department of Economics and Finance, and Executive Director of the New Zealand Institute for the Study of Competition and Regulation;

\- Professor Tony van Zijl, Professor of Accounting and Financial Management, and Director of the Centre for Accounting, Governance and Taxation Research at Victoria University of Wellington;

\- Dr Ralph Lattimore, Senior Fellow with the New Zealand Institute of Economic Research, recently returned from an appointment with the OECD as a Senior Trade Policy Analyst on trade performance of faster emerging markets.

The four new members join Professor Stephen King, Dr John Small, and Dr Rhonda Smith, whose appointments have been renewed.

Lay members assist the Court in cases involving appeals from decisions of the Commerce Commission and in other matters under the Commerce Act.
