A host of new appointments will strengthen the Waitangi Tribunal and help ensure it remains fit for purpose, Māori Development Minister Tama Potaka says.

“As the Tribunal nears its fiftieth anniversary, the appointments coming on board will give it the right balance of skills to continue its important mahi hearing and reporting Māori treaty claims in a timely manner.

“Ensuring we have a range of talented appointees on boards and tribunals is absolutely key to the delivery of better public services and, for the Waitangi Tribunal, supporting the progress of the Treaty-based Crown-Māori relationship.”

The new members, appointed for a three-year term, are:

**Tipene Chrisp** (Whāngai to Rangitāne) has held senior roles across the public service, with extensive knowledge of Waitangi Tribunal hearings and negotiation experience. He is currently General Manager of Rangitāne Tū Mai Rā Trust.

**Philip Crump** has practised law for over 20 years in both Aotearoa New Zealand and the United Kingdom, giving him significant experience of statutory and contractual interpretation, negotiation and dispute resolution procedures. He is currently a board member of NZ on Air.

**Vanessa Eparaima** (Raukawa, Ngāti Tūwharetoa) is an experienced Governor across a range of nonprofit organisations, commercial and investment boards. Her experience includes Treaty negotiator for Ngāti Raukawa.  She is currently the Chairperson of Te Wananga O Aotearoa council.

**Rex Edward Hale** is an author and experienced public servant, including information and data management and experience in preparing oral and written resources to support Tribunal claimants.

**Grant Hadfield** is a Councillor on the Manawatū District Council and serves as Chair of the Chief Executive’s Employment Committee. He has held a range of executive and governance roles, including CEO and Executive Secretary of Manawatū Agricultural and Pastoral Association and former CEO of FarmSafe.

**Kingi Kiriona** (Ngāti Ruanui, Ngāti Kahungunu, Ngāti Apa) is an orator, kapa haka exponent, composer, educator and broadcaster. He is currently a Director of Whakaata Maori and a member of Te Mātāwai.  He is the Managing Director and Founder of Tupu Ora Education and Development Ltd, which supports building Māori capability for iwi and public sector agencies.

**Honourable Ron Mark** served in the New Zealand Army (1971 – 1990) before moving into governance and politics. He has been a respected Member of Parliament, serving as Minister of Defence and Veterans. Mr Mark is currently serving his second term as Mayor of Carterton.

**Professor Tafaoimalo Tologata Leiland Tuala-Warren** is the Dean of Te Piringa Faculty of Law at the University of Waikato, making her Aotearoa New Zealand’s first Pacific Dean of Law. Professor Tuala-Warren has held a range of senior roles over her 26-year career, including becoming the second woman to act as Supreme Court Justice of Samoa. In 2022, she was recognised as a Distinguished Alumni of the University of Waikato.

The five members reappointed to the Waitangi Tribunal are:

**Dr Ruakere Hond** (Taranaki, Ngāti Ruanui) who will be serving a third term.

**Derek Fox** (Ngāti Kahungunu, Ngāti Porou) who will be serving a second term.

**Kim Ngarimu** (Ngāti Porou) who will be serving a third term.

**Dr Hana O’Regan** (Ngāi Tahu) who will be serving a second term.

**Professor Sir Pou Temara** (Ngāi Tūhoe) who will be serving a sixth term.

“Waitangi Tribunal members bring with them a range of knowledge and skills and are appointed for their broad expertise in the matters that are likely to come before the Tribunal.

“I give a warm welcome to the new members as well as those reappointed in their roles. I acknowledge and thank those leaving for their important contributions.”

#### He mema hōu i whakatūngia i te Te Rōpū Whakamana i Te Tiriti o Waitangi

Nā te ikanga mai o ngā toa hou e whakapakari ake i Te Rōpu Whakamana i te Tiriti o Waitangi me te mātua whakarite e noho takatū tonu te Taraipiunara mō te koronga i hangaia ai, te kī a te Minita Whanaketanga Māori Tama Potaka.

“Ka pātata nei te Taraipiunara ki tōna huritau rima tekau, mā ngā toa ka eke mai e here mai i ngā pūkenga e tika ana kia kawea tonutia ai ngā mahi nui whakaharahara nei i ngā nohoanga me ngā whakatakoto pūrongo e pā ana ki ngā kokoraho tiriti a te Māori i roto i ngā whakaritenga wā e tika ana.

Matua ake nei ko te mātua whakarite he whānui ngā momo kopounga whai pūmanawa i runga i ngā poari me ngā taraipiunara mō te whakatutukinga o te rāngai kāwanatanga pai ake nei, ā, mō te taha ki Te Rōpu Whakamana i te Tiriti o Waitangi, ko te tautoko i te pikinga o te hononga Karauna-Māori ka ahu mai i te Tiriti.”

Ko ngā kaiwhiri hou i kopounga mō te toru tau, ko:

**Tīpene Chrisp** (Whāngai ki Rangitāne) kua whai tūranga matua a Tīpene huri noa, huri noa i te rāngai kāwanatanga, kei a ia hoki te mātauranga whānui o ngā nohoanga o Te Rōpu Whakamana i te Tiriti o Waitangi, ka mutu, he taunga hoki ki ngā mahi whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro, kōrero hoki. I tēnei wā ko ia te Tumu Whakahaere Matua o Rangitāne Tū Mai Rā Trust.

**Philip Crump** kua mahi rōia a Philip mō te 20 tau neke atu i roto tahi o Aotearoa me Te Hononga o Ingarangi, nō reira tōna mātau hiranga ake nei o ngā whakamāoritanga ā-ture, ā-kirimana, te whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro, kōrero, me ngā tukanga whakatau tautohetohe. I tēnei wā he mema poari ia i Irirangi Te Motu.

**Vanessa Eparaima** (Raukawa, Ngāti Tūwharetoa) he Tumu Mana Whakahaere puta noa i ngā momo whakahaere hua hoko-kore, ā-arumoni hoki, me ngā poari haumi pūtea.  Tōna wheako he whānui tae atu ki te mahi whakawhitiwhiti whakaaro, kōrero hoki mō Ngāti Raukawa.  I tēnei wā ko te Heamana ia o te kaunihera o Te Wananga O Aotearoa.

**Rex Edward Hale** he kaitito ia, he kaimahi kāwanatanga whai wheako nui hoki ia, tae atu ki te whakahaere mōhiohio me te raraunga, he taunga anō hoki ia i te whakaritenga o ngā rauemi ā-waha, ā-tuhi hoki hei tautoko i ngā kaikokoraho ki te Taraipiunara.

**Grant Hadfield** he Kaikaunihera ia i runga i te Kaunihera ā-Rohe o Manawatū, e tuku wā anō ana ia hei Heamana o te Komiti Mahi o te Tumu Whakahaere. Kua whai mahi ia i ngā momo tūranga tumu mana whakahaere, tae atu ki te Tumu Whakahaere Matua me te Tumu Whakahaere o te Manawatū Agricultural and Pastoral Association, me te Tumu Whakahaere Matua o mua o FarmSafe.

**Kingi Kiriona** (Ngāti Ruanui, Ngāti Kahungunu, Ngāti Apa) he pūkōrero, he tohunga ki te kapa haka, he ringa tito waiata, he kaiwhakaako, he kaipāpaho anō hoki. I tēnei wā he Kaiwhakatere ia o Whakaata Māori, he kaiwhiri hoki ia o Te Mātāwai.  Ko Kīngi te Mana Whakahaere me te Pou Orokohanga o Tupu Ora Education and Development Ltd, e tautoko ana i te tipuranga o te āheinga Māori mā ngā iwi me ngā hinonga karauna.

**Te Hōnore Ron Mark** ka tū ia hei hōia i te ia i Ngāti Tūmatauenga (1971 - 1990) i mua i te whai wāhi atu ki ngā mahi mana whakahaere me te tōrangapū. Kua noho ia hei Mema Pāremata, e noho ana hei Minita o Te Ope Kātua me ngā Ika ā-Whiro. Ko te wāhanga tuarua tēnei kua noho a te Hōnore Ron Mark hei Koromatua o Carterton.

**Te Toihau Tafaoimalo Tologata Leiland Tuala-Warren** ko ia te Manutaki o te Whare Kura Te Piringa i Te Whare Wānanga o Waikato, nō reira, ko te Manutaki Ture tuatahi ia o te Moana-nui-a-Kiwa ki Aotearoa. Kua whai wāhi ia ki ngā momo tūranga pou matua i roto i tōna 26 tau o tana mahi pūmau, oti rā, e mōhiotia ana ia hei wāhine tuarua ki te tū hei Tumu Whakawā o te Kooti Matua o Hāmoa. I te tau 2022, i whakanuia tōna mana hei Pia Taiea o Te Whare Wānanga o Waikato.

Ko ngā kaiwhiri kua kopoua anōtia ki Te Rōpū Whakamana i te Tiriti o Waitangi ko:

**Tākuta Ruakere Hond** (Taranaki, Ngāti Ruanui) ko te tuatorutanga tēnei māna.

**Derek Fox** (Ngāti Kahungunu, Ngāti Porou) ko te tuaruatanga tēnei māna.

**Kim Ngarimu** (Ngāti Porou) ko te tuatorutanga tēnei māna.

**Tākuta Hana O’Regan**  (Ngāi Tahu) ko te tuaruatanga tēnei māna.

**Te Toihau Tā Pou Temara** (Ngāi Tūhoe) ko te tuaonotanga tēnei māna.

“Ka here mai e ngā kaiwhiri o Te Rōpu Whakamana i te Tiriti o Waitangi i te whānui tonu o ngā momo mātauranga, pūkenga hoki, waihoki, ka kopounga rātou mō te whānui o tō rātou tohungatanga i ngā take me i kore e puta ake ki mua ki te aroaro o Te Taraipiunara.

“Nei aku mihi mahana ki ngā kaiwhiri hou tae atu ki ngā mātanga i whai tūranga anō ai. Kāore i ārikarika āku kupu whakamiha ki te hunga ka wehe atu nei, oti rā ki ō rātou takoha whakahirahira mai.”
