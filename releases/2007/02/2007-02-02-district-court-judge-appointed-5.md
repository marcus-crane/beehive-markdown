Whangarei lawyer Noel Douglas Cocurullo has been appointed a District Court Judge with a Family Court warrant, Attorney-General Michael Cullen announced today.

Mr Cocurullo has been practicing as a lawyer for 18 years, beginning  
as a sole practitioner in 1994 after a period of employment with Webb Ross  
Johnson. His early experience included general conveyancing, liquor  
licensing, civil and criminal litigation and family law work. He has  
specialised in criminal and family litigation since 1991, with particular  
emphasis on family work since 1996.

His practice includes counsel for child and counsel to assist roles, Youth Advocate, a significant amount of matrimonial/relationship property work, and some conveyancing. He receives Counsel to Assist briefs and is often appointed to assist in proceedings where litigants identify as Maori. He has served on the  
Auckland District Law Society Council and on various legal aid committees.

Outside the law he has been active in Whangarei One Stop Shop (an  
organisation concerned with the delivery of social services to Northland  
youth), in the Northland CAB and in music. Mr Cocurullo is of  
Ngati Pikiao/Te Arawa descent.

Mr Cocurullo will be sworn in at Whangarei on 12 February 2007.
