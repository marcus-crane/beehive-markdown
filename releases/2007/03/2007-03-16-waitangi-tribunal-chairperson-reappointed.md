Maori Affairs Minister Parekura Horomia today announced the reappointment of Joseph Victor Williams, Chief Judge of the Maori Land Court as Chairperson of the Waitangi Tribunal for a further five years.

In his time as Chairperson of the Waitangi Tribunal Chief Judge Williams has successfully reformed and refocused the inquiry process of the tribunal and significantly reduced the time spent on district inquiries.

Prior to his appointment as Chief Judge of the Maori Land Court, Chief Judge Williams was a founding partner of Walters Williams and Company, a successful law firm where he specialised in environmental, public, and Maori law. While in private practice he worked closely with Maori communities and leaders.

Chief Judge Williams has a sound knowledge and understanding of tikanga Maori and is a well-known and respected member of the judiciary. Chief Judge Williams is of Ngati Pukenga, Te Arawa descent.
