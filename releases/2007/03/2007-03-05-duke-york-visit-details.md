Prime Minister Helen Clark today announced that His Royal Highness the Duke of York will have official engagements in Wellington, Wairarapa, Rotorua, and Auckland during his visit this month.

"His Royal Highness will attend a dinner at Government House in Wellington hosted by His Excellency the Governor-General Anand Satyanand on Thursday 15 March," Helen Clark said.

"The following day the Duke will receive a briefing on trade links between NZ Trade and Enterprise (NZTE) and UK Trade and Investment (UKTI), and visit the Royal New Zealand Army Logistic Regiment at Trentham Camp. The Duke is Colonel-in-Chief of the Regiment.

"I am delighted that His Royal Highness will be present in Rotorua on Saturday 17 March for the ceremony which take place at Te Papiouru Marae, Ohinemutu, to celebrate the gallantry of Lance Sergeant Haane Manahi during World War II.

"The Duke of York will also visit Rotorua Museum, and attend a special gala dinner hosted by Te Arawa in honour of members of the 28th Maori Battalion. On Sunday 18 March, he will visit Mokoia Island and later that day will take part in a re-dedication ceremony at the Auckland War Memorial Museum cenotaph.

"The Duke has engagements in Auckland on Monday 19 March including a briefing by the Guild of Air Pilots and Air Navigators, a visit to Devonport Naval Base, the official opening of new Digital Operating Theatres at Mercy Ascot Hospital, and a reception at the Northern Club hosted by the British New Zealand Trade Council.

On Tuesday 20 March His Royal Highness will give a keynote address to the Opportunity UK Trade and Investment Summit at the SkyCity Convention Centre, and then join the Governor-General for a function at Government House to celebrate International Down's Syndrome Day. Later he will visit McDell Marine boatbuilders at Glendene, and in the evening will attend the Opportunity UK Trade & Investment Summit gala charity dinner at SkyCity," Helen Clark said.
