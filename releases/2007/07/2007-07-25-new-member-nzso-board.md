Prime Minister and Minister for Arts, Culture and Heritage Helen Clark today announced the appointment of Peter Diessl to the Board of the New Zealand Symphony Orchestra (NZSO).

Peter Diessl of Wellington is currently vice-chair of the Chamber Music NZ Board and he belongs to the New Zealand String Quartet Foundation. He has a long history in music management and is director of several companies. He is also Honorary Consul-General for Austria. His three-year term is from 1 August 2007.

“I am very pleased to announce Peter Diessl’s appointment to the NZSO Board,” Helen Clark said.

“His commercial and financial skills and his knowledge of the administration of music in New Zealand will be an asset for the NZSO."

Helen Clark also expressed her gratitude for the many years given to the Board by retiring member Joe Pope.

“During Joe's eleven years on the Board, he has helped the NZSO to develop and maintain a successful marketing focus and budget, " Helen Clark said.

BACKGROUND INFORMATION

Peter Diessl has broad-based experience in top management, communication, finance, investment, accounting and a variety of other fields (music management, international diplomacy, farming, forestry and directorships.)

Former Chair of the New Zealand String Quartet Trust, he is currently Vice-Chair of the Chamber Music New Zealand Board and a member of the New Zealand String Quartet Foundation. He is Honorary Consul-General for Austria.

The objective of the NZSO is to enrich the musical life of New Zealanders by presenting artistically excellent concerts, performed throughout the country and abroad, by a full time professional orchestra of international standing.
