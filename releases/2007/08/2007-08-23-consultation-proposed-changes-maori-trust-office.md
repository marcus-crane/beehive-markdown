The Government is set to undertake consultation with Mâori on proposals to change the Mâori Trustee and Mâori Trust Office, says Mâori Affairs Minister Parekura Horomia.

“The proposed changes have come out of a review of the Mâori Trustee and are intended to help the Trustee to meet future challenges and to deliver services to Mâori’, the Minister said.

The proposals would set up the Mâori Trustee as a stand-alone organisation, separate from Te Puni Kôkiri. They would involve a funding agreement between the Mâori Trustee and the Crown and introduce new public funding reporting requirements.

The Mâori Trustee is currently an appointment conferred on a senior Te Puni Kôkiri staff member and staff of the Mâori Trust Office are Te Puni Kôkiri employees. It is proposed to transfer all present Mâori Trust Office staff to the new stand-alone organisation.

Under the proposals a requirement that the Mâori Trustee review and adjust the interest rates paid on funds held in trust in the Common Fund would be introduced, to take into account appropriate market interest rates.

The Mâori Trustee has a range of statutory duties to do with administering Mâori land and assets on behalf of owners who are not known or who haven’t succeeded to their interests. The Trustee also administers land or assets where the owners have asked the Trustee to do so.

The Trustee also manages funds held in trust for owners, and administers a number of reserves created in the 19th century.

Eleven hui will be held around the country over the next few weeks in areas where the Mâori Trustee has offices and administers land and assets. The proposals will require legislative changes, expected to be in place by July 2008.

Kua reri te kâwanatanga ki te whakahaere kôrerorero ki te Mâori mô ngâ whakaritenga ki te whakarerekç I te Kaitiaki Mâori, me te Tari Kaitiaki Mâori, e kî ana te Minita Take Mâori, a Parekura Horomia.

“ I puta mai ngâ whakaaro whakarerekç I tçtahi arotake i te Kaitiaki Mâori, me te hiahia ki te awhina I te Kaitiaki ki te whakatutuki I ngâ wero kei mua, me te kawe ratonga atu ki te Mâori,” e kî ana te Minita.

Ka whai çnei whakaritenga ki te whakatû I te Kaitiaki Mâori, hei rôpû tû wehe ake ki Te Puni Kôkiri. Ka hangaia he kirimini whakapûtea I waenga I te Kaitiaki Mâori me te Karauna, ka whakamôhiotia çtahi herenga hou hei whakapûrongo pûtea tûmatanui.

I tçnei wâ he whakatûtanga te Kaitiaki Mâori, i tçtahi kaimahi mâtâmua o Te Puni Kôkiri, me ngâ kaimahi a te Kaitiaki Mâori he kaimahi anô a Te Puni Kôkiri. E whakaaro ana ki te whakawhiti atu ngâ kaimahi a te Tari Kaitiaki Mâori ki te rôpû tû wehe hou.

Kei raro anô I te whakaritenga tçtahi here ki runga I te Kaitiaki Mâori ki te arotake, me te whakatika I ngâ auau itareti e utua ana mô ngâ pûtea e pupuri kaitiaki ana I roto I te Pûtea Pâtahi, ka whakamôhiotia, me te whakauru atu I ngâ auau itareti tôtika mô çnei wâ.

Ko ngâ mahinga a te Kaitiaki Mâori he mahinga whânui I raro i te ture, ki te whakahaere I ngâ whenua me ngâ rawa Mâori mô ngâ kaipupuri kâhore I te môhiotia, kahore anô rânei kia kairîwhi ki a râtou pânga whenua. Kei te whakahaere anô te Kaitiaki Mâori I ngâ whenua me ngâ rawa o ngâ kaipupuri kua tono atu ki te kaitiaki ki te whakahaere.

Kai te whakahaere te Kaitaiki I ngâ môni e pupuri tiaki ana mô ngâ kaipupuri whenua, me çtahi whenua râhui I hangaia I te rautau 19.

Te kau mâ tahi ngâ hui ka tû ki te motu, I waenga I ngâ wiki ruarua kei mua, ki ngâ wâhi kei reira ngâ tari a te Kaitiaki e whakahaere ana I ngâ whenua me ngâ rawa. I raro anô I ngâ whakaritenga ka whakaturetia ngâ rerekçtanga, me te wawata ka tutuki tçnei I mua o te marama o Hûrae 2008.
