A new resource launched today will help small tourism operators take the next step in their business development, Tourism Minister Damien O'Connor says.

Mr O'Connor launched the new business development tool at the Tourism Industry Association New Zealand (TIA) head office in Wellington.

Tourism in Action – resources for successful tourism businesses, is a CD and workbook containing eight practical modules, including how to set up a business, develop an idea, and promote a business, which will assist thousands of small tourism operators build better businesses, Mr O'Connor said.

TIA developed the resource in association with New Zealand Trade and Enterprise.

“Creating and developing sustainable tourism businesses is vital to the long-term success of New Zealand’s economy," Mr O'Connor said.

"Congratulations to TIA for producing this valuable tool, which is designed to work with Tourism New Zealand’s publication Give it 100% - an introductory guide to marketing and developing your tourism product.

"The development of these resources demonstrates once again that the tourism sector and the Government work well together to achieve the common aim of ensuring a prosperous New Zealand.

"We must continue to work closely to meet head-on the global and domestic challenges facing the tourism industry, such as how to attract higher spending visitors, develop sustainable businesses, and enhance New Zealand's clean and green image."

These major issues and others affecting the industry are presently being considered by industry leaders and government agencies as part of the New Zealand Strategy Update 2020, which is expected to be out for industry consultation within weeks.
