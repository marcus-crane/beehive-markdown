Transport Minister Annette King today launched an innovative "New Zealand, New Thinking" exhibition in Guangzhou, China.

Acting on behalf of Prime Minister and Arts, Culture and Heritage Minister Helen Clark, Ms King said it was a pleasure to open an exhibition which presents the modern image of New Zealand to the Chinese people.

“I invite the people of Guangzhou and the many visitors to this city to visit the New Zealand New Thinking exhibition in the Guangzhou Grandview Plaza shopping centre to learn more about New Zealand, and to enjoy performances by our modern performance groups Whitireia Performing Arts and Strike”.

The New Zealand, New Thinking exhibition presents an image of modern New Zealand, and utilises interactive, cutting-edge technology to do so.

The New Zealand, New Thinking exhibition opens in Guangzhou during the 101st Canton fair. It is on display on the 5th floor of the Guangzhou Grandview Plaza from 27 April until 6 May. New Zealand, New Thinking has previously visited Beijing, Shanghai and Wuhan.

Prime Minister Helen Clark, launched New Zealand, New Thinking in China via satellite in Beijing on 13 March.
