Women's Affairs Minister Lianne Dalziel is in Japan this week (25-29 September) to deliver the keynote address at the NZ-Japan joint symposium on gender equality in Tokyo.

The symposium aims to deepen dialogue between the two countries on social and economic issues for women.

Other speakers include - from New Zealand: Equal Opportunities Commissioner Dr Judy McGregor, as well as Michael Barnett, chief executive of the Auckland regional Chamber of Commerce and Industry and chair of the Equal Employment Opportunities Trust; and from Japan: Ms Akiko Ide, senior vice president and managing director of the Corporate Citizenship Department of NTT DoCoMo, one of Japan's largest mobile telecommunications companies, Professor Hiroki Sato of the University of Tokyo and member of the Council for Gender Equality, and Ms Reiko Kanda, director of the Research Division, Gender Equality Bureau.

Lianne Dalziel has appointments to meet with the Minister of State for Gender Equality to discuss fertility, women in leadership, women's labour market participation and reconciling work, caring and childcare; and, the Minister of State for Financial Services on the harmonisation of financial reporting standards, international standards and cooperation in securities law, and strengthening economic and trade ties.

While in Japan, Lianne Dalziel will also participate in meetings with business, trade and governmental organisations as part of her Commerce and Small Business portfolios, as part of New Zealand's commitment to strengthening trade and economic ties with Japan.

Lianne Dalziel will meet with – amongst others – officials from Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry including the head of the Small and Medium Enterprise Agency; the chair of the Japan Fair Trade Commission Mr Kazuhiko Takeshima; senior officials from Mitsui & Co Ltd, a large trading corporation that invests in New Zealand mainly in natural resources including oil and gas and dairy, and which has expressed interest in exploring business opportunities in the forestry sector.
