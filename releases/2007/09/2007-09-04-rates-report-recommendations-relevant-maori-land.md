The Independent Inquiry into Local Government Rates has made some significant recommendations relating to Mâori land, says Mâori Affairs Minister Parekura Horomia.

“The Government made public the final report on the inquiry into local government funding last week. I want to acknowledge the significant work that the independent inquiry team has done, particularly in highlighting the issues relating to land covered by Te Ture Whenua Mâori Act 1993.”

“The terms of reference asked the inquiry to consider the impact of rates on land covered by the Act. I welcome the work the panel has done to investigate this matter including: seeking background information on Mâori land, holding 12 hui from Whangarei to Invercargill, seeking a range of oral and written submissions and the assistance of experienced consultants well-versed in Mâori land issues.”

“There are many key messages to take from the report. Overall, the panel concludes that a new and different approach to rating and evaluation is needed. Much Mâori land is productive and rates are being paid on that land by the owners. However, some rates are not being paid on unproductive Mâori land.”

“The report addresses the following key issues: rates rebates and papakâinga housing, the Treaty of Waitangi and rating law,valuation of Mâori land, rates remissions policies, historic legislation changing the status of Mâori land to general land, rating and Mâori land development, training of local government in Mâori land issues and rating exemptions in relation to Mâori land.”

“The government is now considering the full range of the inquiry’s recommendations in detail and will work closely with interested parties to analyse and address issues outlined in the report.”

The full inquiry report is available at www.ratesinquiry.govt.nz. Chapter 13 relates to the ‘Impact of Rates on Land covered by Te Ture Whenua Maori Act’.

Kei te whakaâe te Minita mô ngâ Take Mâori, a Parekura Horomia, he nui tonu çtahi o ngâ tûtohutanga kei roto I te Uiuinga Motuhake ki ngâ Reiti Kâwanatanga Â-rohe, ka whakapâ atu ki te whenua Mâori.

“I pânuitia e te Kâwanatanga te pûrongo whakamutunga mô te Uiuinga ki te pûtea  
whakamôhio I ngâ wâhanga take e whakapâ tata ana ki ngâ whenua kei raro I Te Ture Whenua Mâori 1993.”

“I tonoa e ngâ ture whakahaere kia whakaarotia e te Uiuinga Reiti ngâ whakapâtanga a ngâ reiti ki runga I ngâ whenua kei raro I Te Ture Whenua Mâori 1993. E tautoko ana ahau I ngâ mahi kua oti e te Rôpû ki te rangahau I te take nei, tâpiri atu ki ngâ pârongo takenga mai mô te whenua Mâori, te whakahaere I ngâ hui te kau mâ rua mai I Whangarei ki Murihiku, te rapu tâpaetanga whânui â-waho, â-tuhi, me ngâ âwhinatanga a çtahi tohunga matatau e möhio pai ana ki ngâ take whenua Mâori.”

“ He maha ngâ take nui hei tango mai I te pûrongo. Tçtahi take whânui, kua whakatau e te Rôpû me hangaia he huarahi hou, huarahi rerekç, mô ngâ reiti me ngâ aromatawai. He wâhanga nui tonu o te whenua Mâori kei te whakaputa hua, kei te utua anô e ngâ kaipupuri whenua ngâ reiti. Engari, kahore I te utua ngâ reiti mô ngâ whenua Mâori kahore I te whakaputa hua.”

“ Kua anganui atu te pûrongo ki ngâ kaupapa take nui e whai ake nei: te whakahekenga utu reiti me ngâ whenua papakâinga; te Tiriti o Waitangi me ngâ ture reiti; te whakawâriu I te whenua Mâori; ngâ kaupapa here whakakore utu reiti; ngâ ture hîtori I whekrerekç I te âhua o te whenua Mâori atu ki te whenua whânui; ngâ reiti me te whanaketanga whenua Mâori; te whakangungu I ngâ tari kâwanatanga â-rohe mô ngâ take whenua Mâori; me te whakawâteatanga o ngâ reiti me te whenua Mâori.”

“Kei te whakaaroaro te Kâwanatanga inâianei I ngâ momo tûtohutanga whânui a te Uiuitanga ka mahi tahi ki ngâ rôpû e whaipânga ana ki te tâtari me te whakatika I ngâ take kua whakarârangitia ki roto I te pûrongo.”

Kei te takoto wâtea te pûrongo kei www.ratesinquiry.govt.nz . Ko ûpoko 13 e pâ ana ki ngâ whakapâtanga a ngâ reiti ki runga I ngâ whenua kei raro I Te Ture Whenua Mâori.
