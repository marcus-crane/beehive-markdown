Te Rûnanga o Te Rarawa and the Crown signed an Agreement in Principle for the settlement of Te Rarawa’s historical Treaty claims today.

Minister in Charge of Treaty of Waitangi Negotiations Mark Burton, Minister of Mâori Affairs Parekura Horomia, Associate Minister in Charge of Treaty of Waitangi Negotiations Mita Ririnui and local MP and Associate Minister for Industrial and Regional Development Dover Samuels represented the Crown at the signing in Pangaru.

“This step in the historical Treaty settlement process is the culmination of five years of negotiation between the parties and is a fine testament to the persistence and fortitude of the negotiators. I wish them well in concluding the final stages of the negotiations,” Mr Horomia said.

The Agreement in Principle provides for the transfer of 10 sites of cultural and historical significance to Te Rarawa. It also includes commercial redress comprising a quantum of $20 million, the opportunity to purchase 29 percent of the Aupouri Forest and other Crown properties, and a commitment by the Crown to explore buying two Landcorp farms that Te Rarawa will have the opportunity to acquire over time.

A key feature of the agreement is redress that provides for Te Rarawa’s ongoing relationship with the Crown through an annual meeting between Te Rarawa and the Minister of Mâori Affairs (or nominee). “This instrument reflects the commitment of the parties to a positive, future-focused ongoing relationship,” said Mr Horomia.

The agreement also includes a Whenua Ngahere package which promotes Te Rarawa participation in the management of DOC estate in their rohe. “I congratulate Te Rarawa on development of this proposal and look forward to seeing this integrated management approach in action,” said Mr Ririnui.

“Te Rarawa is part of the Muriwhenua grouping of iwi and hapû which have remained committed to finalising their settlements. This will enable grassroots people to keep moving forward,” said Mr Samuels.

The next step in the historical settlement process is for Te Rarawa and the Crown to develop and finalise a Deed of Settlement which will be presented to the people of Te Rarawa for ratification before it is signed.

I te rangi nei i hainatia e te Karauna me Te Rûnanga o Te Rarawa tçtahi Kirimini Tikanga mô te whakataunga o ngâ kerçme hîtori Tiriti o Te Rarawa.

Te rôpû mângai a te Karauna i te hui haina ko, te Minita Whakahaere i ngâ Whiriwhiringa i raro i te Tiriti o Waitangi, a Mark Burton, te Minita mô ngâ Take Mâori, a Parekura Horomia, te Minita Tuarua Whakahaere i ngâ Whiriwhiringa i raro i te Tiriti o Waitangi, a Mita Ririnui, me te Mema Paremata o te Rohe, te Minita Tuarua mô te Whanaketanga Ahumahi me te ngâ Rohe, a Dover Samuels.

“Tçnei hîkoi i roto i ngâ whakaritenga whakataunga Tiriti,kô te whakatinanatanga o ngâ whakawhitinga whakaaro mô te rima tau i waenga i ngâ rôpû, â, koia nei te whakaaturanga pai o te taikaha me te pakari o ngâ kaiwhakawhiti kôrero. Ka tukuna aku mihi pai ki a râtou kia whakatutuki ai ngâ wâhanga whakamutunga o ngâ whakawhitinga kôrero,” e kî ana a Horomia.

Kei roto i te Kirimini Tikanga te whakawhiwhi i ngâ wâhi tapu, hîtori rongonui, 10, ki a Te Rarawa. Ka tâpiri anô te utu whakamahea arumoni, te pûtea $20 miriona, he huarahi wâtea ki te hoko i te 29 ôrauo te Ngahere Râkau o Aupouri me çtahi ake whenua Karauna, me tçtahi tohu piripono a te Karauna ki te rangahau i te hoko i ngâ pâmu a Landcorp e rua, hei hokona mâ Te Rarawa hei te wâ.

Ko tçtahi o ngâ wâhanga take nui o te kirimini ko te whakamahea ka whakawhiwhi kia mau tonu te honohononga a Te Rarawa ki te Karauna i runga i ngâ huihuinga ia tau i waenga i Te Rarawa me te Minita mô ngâ Take Mâori (tôna tautapa rânei). “Ka whakaata e te kirimini nei te piriponotanga o ngâ rôpû ki tçtahi honohononga inetahi, arotahi whakamua, ka haere tonu,” i kî a Horomia.

Kei roto anô i te kirimini tçtahi pûhera Whenua Ngahere e whakatairanga ana i te whaiwâhitanga a Te Rarawa ki ngâ tikanga whakahaere i ngâ rawa a Te Papa Atawhai i roto i tâ râtou rohe. “ Ka whakamihi ahau ki a Te Rarawa mô te whanaketanga o tçnei kaupapa, ka titiro whakamua ki tçnei whakahaere whakakotahi e mahia ana,” i kî a Ririnui.

“Ko Te Rarawa tçtahi o ngâ iwi me ngâ rôpû kohinga o Muriwhenua i te whanga roa tonu ki te whakatutuki i a râtou whakataunga. Mâ kônei ka âhei ngâ tangata taketake ki te haere whakamua tonu,” i kî a Samuels.

Ko te hîkoi panuku i roto i ngâ whakaritenga hîtori, ko te whanaketanga me te whakaotinga mâ Te Rarawa me te Karauna i tçtahi Kirimini Whakataunga hei whakatakoto ki mua o ngâ tangata o Te Rarawa kia whakamanatia i mua o te hainatanga.
