One of New Zealand's premier visitor destinations, Cape Reinga (Te Rerenga Wairua), in the Far North, is to get a $6.5 million government-funded make over as part of Budget 2007.

"Located a the very tip of New Zealand where the Pacific Ocean and Tasman Sea meets, Cape Reinga is home to some of New Zealand's rarest plants and animals, and is important to Maori as the last departure point for spirits on the homeward journey to Hawaiiki," Associate Conservation Minister Mahara Okeroa said.

"With investment, the Labour-led government believes Cape Reinga can grow further as a destination delivering considerable economic and social benefits to the Far North. Budget 2007 delivers that investment as part of a broader programme to protect and enhance New Zealand's special places."

Budget 2007 enables DOC to spend $6.5m over two years on the redevelopment of visitor facilities at the site, such as car parks, eco-friendly toilets, power generation, new roading, walking tracks and replanting through reallocation of scheduled recreational opportunity capital funding.

This work will help prepare the area for the development of a visitor centre in future, which the Far North community is fund raising for.

"This redevelopment project for Cape Reinga has been under discussion by the Department of Conservation, local iwi, tourism operators and Enterprise Northland for six years," Mr Okeroa said.

"Transit New Zealand is intending to reseal State Highway 1 to Cape Reinga this year, and visitor numbers to the site are expected to rise to more than 200,000 a year. The redevelopments made possible by Budget 2007 will help the Far North seize this opportunity."

Work will begin on the redevelopment before the end of the year.

### $6.5m hanga whakapai mô Te Rerenga Wairua miharo rawa atu

Kua whiwhi e tçtahi o ngâ wâhi manuhiri tâpoi rongonui o Aotearoa, e Te Rerenga Wairua o te Taitokerau, he wâhanga o te Tahua Pûtea 2007, e $6.5 miriona o te pûtea kâwanatanga hei hanga whakapai i taua wâhi.

“ Kei te tihi o Aotearoa tçnei wâhi, kei konei te tûtakitanga o te Moananui-â-Kiwa me te Moana Tâpokapoka â Tâwhaki, ko Te Rerenga Wairua tçrâ, he kâinga kei reira anô çtahi o ngâ tipu me ngâ kararehe onge, he wâhi whakahirahira mô te Mâori i te mea ko konei te wâhi wehenga whakamutunga atu o a râtou wairua e hoki ana ki te kâinga, ki Hawaiki Pâmamao,” e kî ana te Minita Tuarua mô te Papa Atawhai, a Mahara Okeroa.

“ E pono ana te kâwanatanga Reipa-ârahi, mâ tçnei haumitanga ka tipu ake a Te Rerenga Wairua hei wâhi whanake painga ôhanga, painga hapori nui tonu mô te Taitokerau. E whakawhiwhi ana e te Tahua Pûtea 2007, taua haumitanga hei wâhanga o tçtahi kaupapa whânui ake, ki te tiaki, me te whakakaha ake i ngâ wâhi tino pai motuhake o Aotearoa.”

Mâ Tahua Pûtea 2007 ka âhei Te Papa Atawhai ki te whakapau i te $6.5m mô te rua tau, ki te whanake anô i nga wâhi manaaki i ngâ manuhiri kei reira, pçrâ i ngâ wâhi tûnga waka, ngâ whare paku wairâkau, te huri hiko, he rori hou, ngâ ara haere, me te tanu tipu i raro i te kaupapa tohatoha anô i te pûtea haupû rawa huarahi wâtea hâkinakina kua whakarârangitia. Ko çnei mahi ka ârahi ki te whakareri i te wâhi e whanaketia ana mô tçtahi pokapû manaaki manuhiri ka hangaia hei ngâ wâ kei mua. Kei te kohikohi moni ngâ hapori o te Taitokerau mô tçnei kaupapa.

Hei tâ Okeroa, “ E ono tau a Te Papa Atawhai, ngâ iwi kâinga, ngâ kaiwhakahaere tâpoi, me Enterprise Northland, e whiriwhiri ana i tçnei kaupapa whanaketanga anô, mô te Rerenga Wairua.”

“ Ka whai a Ararau Aotearoa ki te whakahou i Huarahi Matua 1, atu ki te Rerenga Wairua i tçnei tau, me te whakaaro ko te pikinga maha ake o ngâ manuhiri ka haere ki taua wâhi, e neke atu ana ki te 200,000 â-tau. Ko ngâ whanaketanga e whakatinanatia nei e te Tahua Pûtea 2007, koia nei ka âwhina i te Taitokerau ki te hopu atu ki ngâ huarahi ka wâtea mai.”  
Ka tîmata ngâ mahi whanaketanga anô i mua o te mutunga o tçnei tau.
