1.The review will include among other things:  
aa brief review of previous work undertaken since 1990 regarding tertiary education governance in New Zealand;  
ba ‘stocktake’ of the present governance position in the New Zealand tertiary education sector, including present initiatives designed to improve governance capability and effectiveness;  
cconsideration of the partnership principles of the Treaty of Waitangi, principles of good governance, academic freedom and institutional autonomy; the tertiary education strategy, fiscal risks to the government and the government’s policies on stakeholder representation; and  
dreview of the relationships and roles of Chief Executives and Councils.

2.The reviewer will produce by the end of March 2003 a report that:  
aestablishes good governance practice for the New Zealand public tertiary education sector;  
bidentifies various options for new or changed governance structure(s) that:  
iclearly articulates the roles and relationships of Councils, Council members, key committees (if needed), Management and Academic Boards;  
iiensure that all the elements of good corporate governance are effectively integrated into a coherent corporate approach by individual TEIs;  
iiiprovide for mechanisms which ensure appropriate alignment with and maximise the chances of success of government tertiary education strategies;  
ivconsider whether a ‘one size fits all’ approach is appropriate;

cadvises on optimal ways of developing overall governance capability; and

didentifies a preferred option for governance in New Zealand tertiary education institutions.

3.A reference group will be appointed by the Associate Minister of Education (Tertiary Education) to assist the review process. The group’s role will be to provide a New Zealand context for the reviewer. It is envisaged that it would meet at least twice with the reviewer and provide comment on a draft report. The reference group will be made up of key stakeholders represented on each TEI Council.
