Statistics Minister Darren Hughes announced today that four new members have joined the Advisory Committee on Official Statistics (ACOS).  They are Dr Colin Tukuitonga (Chief Executive - Ministry of Pacific Island Affairs), Ria Earp (Chief Executive Officer - Mary Potter Hospice), Professor Alan Lee (Professor of Statistics - Auckland University) and Rob Davison (Executive Director - Economic Service Meat and Wool New Zealand).

ACOS is an independent body with a mandate to provide the Minister with advice on key official statistics produced by government. The committee is chaired by Hon David Caygill, Chair of the Electricity Commission and Associate Commerce Commissioner.

In welcoming the new members to the committee Mr Hughes said, "Official Statistics provide a window on the work and performance of government. They are a cornerstone of democracy and provide the public with confidence in good governance. As our nation changes, it is important that the statistics we collect are relevant and useful. ACOS helps to ensure this happens.”

In addition to the Chair, the 10 committee members are:

- Professor Richard Bedford (Deputy Vice Chancellor, Population Studies, Waikato University)
- Dr Peter Bushnell (Deputy Secretary to The Treasury)
- Rob Davison (Executive Director Economic Service Meat and Wool New Zealand)
- Ria Earp (Chief Executive Officer Mary Potter Hospice)
- Don Gray (Deputy Chief Executive Ministry of Social Development)
- Dr Bryan Jenkins (Chief Executive Environment Canterbury)
- Professor Alan Lee (Professor of Statistics Department of Statistics Auckland University)
- Phil O'Reilly (Chief Executive Business New Zealand)
- Roger Procter (Group Manager Ministry of Economic Development)
- Dr Colin Tukuitonga (Chief Executive Ministry of Pacific Island Affairs)
- The Government Statistician Geoff Bascand is an ex-officio member of ACOS.
