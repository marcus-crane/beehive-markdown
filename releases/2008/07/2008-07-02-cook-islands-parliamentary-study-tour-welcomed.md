A visit from members of the Parliament of the Cook Islands provides an opportunity to acknowledge the special relationship that New Zealand has with the Cook Islands, Pacific Island Affairs Minister Luamanuvao Winnie Laban said today.

"Our close connection with the Cook Islands is founded on historical ties, our unique constitutional arrangements, and the number of people of Cook Islands ethnicity living in New Zealand who make up around 22 per cent of our Pacific population.

"This visit is an opportunity for the Parliamentary Study Tour is to gain an understanding of the functioning of New Zealand's Parliament, and take this knowledge back to the Cook Islands," Laban said.

While in New Zealand, the Cook Islands delegation will meet with a range of people, including the Speaker and Clerk of the House of Representatives, the Foreign Affairs, Defence and Trade Select Committee, and both government and opposition members of Parliament.

Members of the delegation are:

• Cassey Tereapii Eggelton, Deputy Speaker of the Parliament of the Cook Islands, Democratic Party MP for Matavera

• Pastor John David Tangi, Democratic Party MP for Tupapa-Maraerenga

• William (Smiley) Heather, Democratic Party Whip, Associate Minister of Works, MP for Ruaau

• Albert (Peto) Nicholas, Cook Islands Party Parliamentary Whip, MP for

Avatiu, Ruatonga & Palmerston Island
