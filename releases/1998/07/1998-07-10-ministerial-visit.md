"The positive results from modernising and placing New Zealand's air navigation services on a commercial footing, will be the focus of a visit to this country by the Minister of Communications and Transport for the Republic of Zambia, Reverend the Honourable Anoshi Chipawa, " Minister of Transport Hon Maurice Williamson announced today.

Reverend the Honourable Anoshi Chipawa will visit New Zealand from 14 - 20 July as guest of the New Zealand Government and the Airways Corporation of New Zealand.

"The Minister's main interest is in the upgrading of air navigation services in Zambia and in this regard he will be having discussions with the Airways Corporation," the Minister said.

"The Airways Corporation has a successful international group providing consulting services in many countries world-wide, including Southern Africa.

"Minister Chipawa will meet with key aviation industry participants, including Air New Zealand and the Civil Aviation Authority, to gain an overview of the New Zealand regulatory and service environment," Mr Williamson said.

A round of meetings to explore wider opportunities between the two countries has been scheduled for the Minister, with Telecom New Zealand, New Zealand Post and the Meteorological Service of New Zealand.

Minister Chipawa will be accompanied by Mr James Zulu, Assistant Secretary (Air) from the Ministry of Communications and Transport, and Mr Robinson Nkonde a Director from the Office of the President of Zambia.
