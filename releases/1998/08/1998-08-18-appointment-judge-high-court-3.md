The Attorney-General, Rt Hon D.A.M. Graham, announced today the appointment of John Richard Wild QC as a Judge of the High Court.

Justice Wild was educated at Nelson College and graduated from Victoria University. He joined the firm of Bell Gully & Co in 1967 as a solicitor. In 1969 he was awarded the Timothy Cleary Memorial Prize.

He commenced practice at the independent bar in 1976 and was appointed a Queen's Counsel in 1993.

He has been a member of the Council of the Bar Association since 1992 and is currently its President. He is an author of McGechan on Procedure. He has been active in Law Society affairs as a contributor to its continuing education programme. He is currently serving on the Law Society Committee on Barrister's Rules and is a trustee of the Douglas Wilson Advocacy Scholarship Trust.

Justice Wild is involved in the Anglican Church, and is a synod representative of St Mary's, Karori.

He is a former treasurer of the Alpine Club and he is still active in the club's affairs. He has been a keen mountaineer all his life and has climbed in most of the countries in South America, in the Chinese and Pakistan Karakoram, the European Alps and extensively in New Zealand. He is a keen runner.

The new Judge's appointment is temporary in the first instance, pending the next vacancy. He will sit in Wellington.
