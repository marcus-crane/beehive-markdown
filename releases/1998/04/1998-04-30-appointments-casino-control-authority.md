Internal Affairs Minister Jack Elder today announced a number of appointments to the Casino Control Authority.

Mr Paul Cooney has been appointed as a member for a three year term from 29 April 1998.

Mr Cooney is a Tauranga barrister and solicitor specialising in local government, resource management and tribunal law. He has been a Commissioner for resource consent applications and an advisor on the Treaty of Waitangi.

In addition, Mr Elder announced the reappointment of Mr Ronald Irvine as Chairman of the CCA for a further one year term, effective from 29 April 1998.

Mr Michael Cox has been reappointed as a member for a one year term as from the same date.

Other members of the Authority are Mr Peter Dew, Ms Sharon Opai, and Mr Bruce Martin.

The function of the Authority, as set out in section 16 of the Casino Control Act, are principally to:

\- consider casino premises and operators' licence applications and set any licence conditions;

\- consider and determine appeals relating to certificates of approval for casino staff;

\- determine policy in relation to the supervision and inspection of casinos;

\- advise the Minister of matters relating to the administration of the Act and regulations made under the Act; and,

\- approve casino games and rules.
