Economic Development Minister Steven Joyce has congratulated WhereScape Software for winning the coveted Supreme Award at the 50th annual New Zealand International Business Awards last night.

WhereScape is a data warehouse automation software provider with customers in 26 countries, including the United States which is its largest market.

“WhereScape has had a global mindset from day one and an ambition to achieve international success. It is a resilient, flexible and innovative company with excellent growth prospects,” Mr Joyce says. 

“The winners of these awards are great New Zealand businesses from a diversified range of industries that are competing and succeeding on the world stage. I am very optimistic about the future for internationally focused Kiwi companies and New Zealand’s export sector overall.”

As well as winning the Supreme Award, WhereScape won the Best Medium Business award.

Two companies, Haka Tours and Timely, jointly won Best Emerging Business and Gallagher the Best Large Business. Straker Translations, New Zealand Mint and Gallagher respectively took home awards for excellence in innovation, marketing, and design, while Ngai Tahu Tourism won the He kai kei aku ringa for Maori Excellence in Export award, and Xero the Excellence in Leadership award.

“As well as developing new skills and technologies, the winning companies are creating jobs and re-investing profits here in New Zealand. Their success contributes to this country’s achievements and our long term prosperity,” Mr Joyce says.

This year marks the 50th anniversary of the prestigious New Zealand International Business Awards, which celebrate the success of New Zealand businesses on the world stage and are run by New Zealand Trade and Enterprise with strategic partner ANZ.

For more information about the winners and the awards please visit: [www.nziba.co.nz](http://www.nziba.co.nz)

**About the New Zealand International Business Awards**

The New Zealand International Business Awards are run by New Zealand Trade and Enterprise, and celebrate the success of New Zealand businesses on the world stage.

This year marks the 50th anniversary of the awards and in that time many of New Zealand’s most iconic companies have been celebrated, including Tonka, Tip Top and the Apple and Pear Marketing Board (ENZA).

ANZ has been the awards’ strategic partner since 2009. The awards other sponsors are KPMG, MFAT, Callaghan Innovation, Special Group, Kotahi, MBIE, Te Puni Kōkiri and Treasury.
