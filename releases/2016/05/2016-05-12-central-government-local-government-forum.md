Central and local government come together in Wellington today for the 22nd forum involving the two sectors Local Government Minister Peseta Sam Lotu-Iiga says.

“The Forum provides the highest level of engagement between central and local government, and is highly valued. It will be jointly chaired by the Prime Minister and the President of Local Government New Zealand, Lawrence Yule,” Mr Lotu-Iiga says.

Nine Ministers will attend the forum, as well as Local Government New Zealand National Council members who are mayors, councillors and regional council chairs from around New Zealand.

“The forum will reflect on the three priorities from the 2015 Forum – creating strong regional economies, resilient infrastructure and ensuring resilient communities across New Zealand.

“We will discuss my Better Local Services reforms and how it can deliver better value services for ratepayers,” Mr Lotu-Iiga says.

“We will also look at the challenges emerging in infrastructure and investment to grow strong regional economies – and how best to overcome those issues,” Mr Lotu-Iiga says.

The forum is being held at Premier House in Wellington.
