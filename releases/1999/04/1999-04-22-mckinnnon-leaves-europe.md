Foreign Minister Don McKinnon heads to Europe on Friday morning for meetings focused on Nigeria, APEC and the New Zealand-Europe relationship.

Mr McKinnon will attend a Commonwealth Ministerial Action Group (CMAG ) meeting in London. Mr McKinnon is Deputy Chair of the CMAG Group, which will be considering the question of Nigeria's full membership of the Commonwealth, following the election of President Obasanjo.

Mr McKinnon will also visit Moscow, where he will meet with Russian Ministers including Prime Minister Primakov. Mr McKinnon will be discussing bilateral issues but will also brief Ministers on New Zealand's chairing of APEC. While in Moscow, Mr McKinnon will lay a wreath at the Tomb of the Unknown Solider to mark ANZAC Day.

Mr McKinnon will also visit Cyprus - the first visit to Cyprus by a New Zealand Foreign Minister. While in Cyprus Mr McKinnon will meet with President Clerides and with New Zealander Dame Ann Hercus, Chief of Mission of the UN Office in Cyprus.

In Germany, Mr McKinnon will participate in consultations with his German counterpart, Joschka Fischer. Germany currently holds the Presidency of the European Union.

Before returning to New Zealand, Mr McKinnon will visit Jakarta to meet with senior Indonesian Ministers, to discuss the situation in East Timor and broader New Zealand - Indonesian relations.
