Associate Health Minister, Hon Tuariki Delamere, leaves for Washington tomorrow to attend the International Policy Conference on Children and Tobacco.

The conference, sponsored by several US senators, as well as the American Cancer Society, will be attended by health officials and politicians from all over the world.

It will address the problems associated with tobacco use among children and then move on to consider the successful policies used by different nations to overcome those problems.

A highlight will be the conference dinner, to be addressed by US Vice-President, Al Gore.

While in the the US, Mr Delamere intends to take the opportunity to hold discussions with officials from the US Food and Drug Administration about the efficacy of procedures for testing food for genetically-modified organisms and he will contunie those discussiopns with scientists at Washington State University.

The Minister returns on Tuesday March 23.
