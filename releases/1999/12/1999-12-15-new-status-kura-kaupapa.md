Dannevirke’s Te Kura Kaupapa o Tamaki Nui a Rua will be able to accept secondary students from next year, Associate Education Minister Parekura Horomia said today.

The school’s application for composite status has been approved by the Minister of Education and will be gazetted tomorrow.

"I am delighted that my first announcement as a new Minister is one which is so positive for te reo Maori education," Mr Horomia said today.

"The decision to approve the application reflects the Government’s commitment to education in te reo Maori and is in response to the growing demand for full Maori immersion secondary schooling in the Dannevirke area.

"Te reo Maori education is a thriving area which the Government is keen to support and help flourish.

"There are now 59 kura kaupapa Maori with 4,800 students. Of these, 14 are wharekura (composite) offering secondary education.

"One aim I have is to increase the number of Maori speakers with teaching qualifications so that kura have access to a large pool of quality teachers to meet their needs," Parekura Horomia said.

He Tohu Hou mo te Kura Kaupapa

Kua whakaetia, nga tauira kura tuarua ki Te Kura Kaupapa o Tamaki Nui a Rua, a te tau e heke mai nei, koia nei te korero, i te rangi nei, a Parekura Horomia, te Minita Awhina mo te Matauranga.

Kua whakaetia te tohu wharekura ki a ratau e te Minita mo te Matauranga, ara, kai apopo ka kahititia.

"Ka nui toku koa, koia nei te whakapaoho tuatahi, aku te Minita hou, ara, a pa ana ki te hapai i to tatau reo i roto i te matauranga," te korero mai a Te Parekura.

"Kai tenei whakaetanga e kitea te pumautanga a te Kawanatanga ki te matauranga ki roto i te reo Maori, e arotia ai enei ahuatanga ki nga tamariki kura tuarua ki te rohe o Tamaki Nui a Rua.

"E tino kaha haere ana nga mahi matauranga ki te reo Maori, e tino kaika ana hoki te Kawanatanga ki te tautoko i era ahuatanga. Kua eke ki te 59 nga kura kaupapa Maori, e 4800 a ratau tauira. Ki era kura, 14 era kua Wharekura, ara, e whai matauranga ai nga tauira kura tuarua ki reira.

"Ko toku tumanako, kia nui noa atu era o tatau e matau nei ki te reo, ki te whai tohu akoranga, e ea tika ai nga wawata a nga kura e kimi nei i etehi kaiako mo ratau,” koia nei te korero mai a Te Parekura.
