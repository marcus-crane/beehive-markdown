Minister of Biosecurity and Wigram MP, Jim Anderton opened the new premises of Fast Cargo International in Woolston and unveiled a plaque to mark the occasion. He said that the success was a tribute to the owners and the thirty staff in the company around New Zealand as well as the rapid growth of trade.

"Everyone associated with this firm is contributing to New Zealand's economy. You are part of the trade we need with the world to improve and enjoy our first-world way of life. Freight is expanding quickly in New Zealand," Jim Anderton said at the opening.

"It's a pleasure to see how this business has developed. It started out only in 1994 and in Christchurch in 1998, when it had just one staff member in rented office space. When it moved in 2004 into premises with eight thousand square feet of space I'm sure visitors thought you must have had a contract with the Canterbury rugby union to store the trophy cabinets. But with this move adding another ten thousand square feet, it's looking more like the All Blacks' trophy cabinet.
