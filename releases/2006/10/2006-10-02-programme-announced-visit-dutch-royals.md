Prime Minister Helen Clark today announced the programme for the official visit to New Zealand by the Prince of Orange, Prince Willem-Alexander, and his wife Princess Maxima of the Netherlands, from 31 October to 4 November.

On Tuesday 31 October the couple will visit Parliament, attend a welcome ceremony at Government House and meet the Governor-General, lay a wreath at the National War Memorial, visit the Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa, and attend an official dinner hosted by the Governor-General.

The following day they will spend time in the Nelson region including visits to the Abel Tasman National Park, Woollaston Estates winery, the Cawthron Laboratory and the Museum of Wearable Art and Classic Cars, before returning to Wellington and attending a reception for members of the Dutch community.

In Christchurch on Thursday 2 November they will visit Antarctica New Zealand and the International Antarctic Centre, the University of Canterbury and the Christchurch Arts Centre. They will also attend a reception for members of the Dutch community.

In Auckland on Friday 3 November they will visit Selwyn College, attend a business lunch, visit Designer Textiles Ltd to see fabric and garments produced from New Zealand merino wool and attend an official dinner hosted by the Government.

The following day they will visit Ons Dorp retirement village in Henderson and then later attend a reception for members of the Dutch community before departing on Saturday afternoon.
