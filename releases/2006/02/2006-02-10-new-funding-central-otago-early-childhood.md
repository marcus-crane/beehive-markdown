Early childhood education in Central Otago is chalking up another gain with $40,000 in government funding.

Labour MP Mahara Okeroa is welcoming the grant money announced this week by Education Minister Steve Maharey.

"Planning grants towards planning two new early childhood education centres are going to City Impact Church (getting $25,000) and Barnardos in Cromwell (getting $15,000)," said Mahara Okeroa.

The centres for families in Central Otago seeking to access early childhood education (ECE) was funded through the government's Discretionary Grants Scheme, which provides annual capital assistance to community-based early childhood centres and will allocate $20.8 million nationwide to new and expanded services in 2005 /06.

“Good quality early childhood education makes a significant contribution to children's development and educational achievements," said Mahara Okeroa.

"This funding means more children can get quality ECE services – especially children from low socio-economic, rural and isolated communities.”

The Discretionary Grants Scheme targets children who would otherwise be less likely to participate, but who are known to get the greatest benefits from access to ECE.

"This funding will increase access to early childhood education for local children from July 2007, when 20 hours free early childhood education for 3 and 4 year old children in teacher-led services begins," said Mahara Okeroa.
