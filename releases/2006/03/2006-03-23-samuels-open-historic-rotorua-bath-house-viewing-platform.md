Hon Dover Samuels Associate Minister of Tourism is to open the reinstated historic Bath House  
viewing platform at the Rotorua Museum of Art and History, this Friday the 24th March at 5.30pm.

The Rotorua Bathhouse was opened in 1908 as the Government’s first investment in tourism  
infrastructure. Today it is classified as a Category One building by the Historic Places Trust and  
is the home of the Rotorua Museum of Art and History. The Bathhouse is an iconic architectural  
feature of Rotorua and is an important part of the history of tourism in New Zealand.

"Rotorua continues to be one of New Zealand’s most popular visitor destinations attracting approximately 575,000 international and 960,000 domestic visitors a year"", said Minister Samuels

As part of Minister Samuels visit to Rotorua he will also meet with The Chair and CEO of Te Puia (Formerly New Zealand Maori Arts And Crafts Institute) to discuss redevelopment plans for the site.

The redevelopment will cost a total of $17.5m (GST excl.); made up of cash reserves accumulated  
through their tourism trading activities, and the borrowing approved by the Minister of Finance.

Te Puia will use this funding to:

·build a new visitor centre and multi-media gallery ($2.4m);  
·replace the existing shop ($2.0m);  
·build an entrance tower ($1.3m);  
·replace the carving and weaving schools ($2.1m); and  
·create new and enhanced interpretation in the Whakarewarewa valley ($1.6m).

Minister Samuels also has responsibilities as Associate Minister of Housing and in this capacity  
he will visit the Fordlands Community Renewal Project, “Fordlands is an innovative project  
that is trialing new ideas in state housing. It involves a whole range of new, well designed  
housing to meet the needs of our future generations, and will assist in creating a real  
sense of community pride,”said Mr Samuels
